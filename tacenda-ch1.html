Chapter One
The Start of Us
Kendrick

“Happy birthday, my dearest Solenne!”

The cheerful voice belonged to a woman I didn’t know, leaning toward the girl in the huge blue dress. She was standing on a chair, just tall enough to reach her candles.

“Happy birthday, baby!” said the man at her side, smiling warmly—I guessed he was her dad.

The birthday girl grinned so wide I thought her cheeks might split. “Thank you, mummy! Thank you, daddy!”

Ugh. Annoying. I just wanted to go home.

I tugged on the hem of my mom’s dress, trying to get her attention. “Kenzy, I told you to behave,” she said, looking down at me.

“I want to go home,” I mumbled, frowning.

She shook her head, bending down so we were eye to eye. “Kenzy, we can’t go home yet. The party’s not over.”

“But—” I started, then thought better of it.

“I told you there’s a lot of food here, right? That’s why you wanted to come with us,” she added. I just nodded. Had I really wanted to come? Not exactly. I didn’t care about the food, and this party wasn’t my choice. My mom had decided I was coming, and that was that.

I huffed quietly, glaring at the blue dress, the candles, and the girl whom I don’t even know.

“Come on, Kenzy,” my mom held my hand. I already knew what was coming. I really did not want to greet her.

“Do I have to?” I groaned.

“Yes, anak, now let’s go.”

I pouted, sighing, still not moving. Just then, my dad stared at me with those eyes.

“Don’t do this here, Kendrick,” he whispered, but I could tell he was mad. I looked at my mom, but she just smiled at me, as usual.

Reluctantly, I walked with them, dragging my feet across the floor like the world’s slowest turtle. Solenne looked up at me, her wide eyes sparkling in the candlelight, and for a second… my annoyance faltered. She was smiling, pure and bright, completely unaware of the storm of irritation—and something else—brewing inside me.

I cleared my throat. “Uh… happy birthday, Solenne,” I mumbled, kicking at the wooden pillar beside me.

Before I could retreat, my mom suddenly handed me a small paper bag. A gift and attached to it was a card that read: Happy 6th birthday, Solenne. Wishing you a happy birthday, from Kendrick Savatier.

What? From me?

I blinked, staring at it, then looked up at my mom. “This is not from me,” I said flatly.

Solenne looked at me, her brows slightly furrowed in confusion, while her mom glanced between us with a polite smile.

My mom let out an awkward laugh, the kind that didn’t sound natural at all. She shifted in place, clearly flustered, like she didn’t know what to do with herself.

“Just give it to her, Kendrick,” my dad’s voice came from behind me, calm but firm.

I sighed, defeated, and turned back to Solenne. Slowly, I held out the bag. “Here,” I muttered, avoiding her eyes.

Her face lit up instantly. “For me?” she asked, her voice soft and excited.

I shrugged. “Apparently.”

She took it carefully, like it was something precious, and smiled at me—wide, genuine, and a little too bright. “Thank you, Kendrick!” Before I could react, she suddenly stepped forward and wrapped her arms around me.

I froze.

Completely.

My brain stopped working. My hands stayed stiff at my sides, unsure of what they were even supposed to do. I could feel the softness of her dress, the faint smell of something sweet—like vanilla or cake—and the warmth of her small arms around me.

What… is she doing?

My eyes darted around, hoping someone would explain this to me. My mom was smiling like this was the cutest thing she had ever seen. My dad looked way too amused.

Slowly, awkwardly, I lifted one hand… and then dropped it again. I didn’t know how to hug back.

She pulled away after a second, still smiling like nothing just happened. Like, she didn’t just completely ruin my ability to function.

“Thank you,” she said again, softer this time.

I swallowed, looking anywhere but at her. “Yeah… whatever.”

But my ears felt hot. My chest felt weird.

❦
“Hi, Kendrick!” I heard Solenne’s tiny voice chirp behind me.

I closed my eyes. Not again. I thought I was finally done with her.

Slowly, I turned around, still biting into the marshmallow skewered with a hot dog on a stick. I chewed lazily, taking my time on purpose, hoping she’d just… go away.

She didn’t.

“What?” I mumbled, mouth half full, glancing down at her.

She stood there, hands clasped behind her back, still wearing that bright, impossible smile. Like she had every intention of staying. Great.

“What do you want?” I asked, still chewing. “You,” she said, smiling like it was the most obvious thing in the world. I frowned, confused. “Me?” I pointed at myself.

She nodded eagerly, her eyes lighting up. “I want to be friends with you!” I stared at her for a moment. Then I looked away. “Well… I don’t.” Her smile faltered. “What?”

“I said I don’t want to be friends with you,” I repeated, more clearly this time.

Silence.

When I looked back at her, her eyes were already glistening, like she was trying really hard not to cry. I sighed, scratching the back of my neck. Is having friends really that important to her? I don’t even have friends. I don’t plan to have any. I don’t want to.

I didn’t want to get in trouble for making a girl cry, so I had no choice. I let out a long sigh. “Fine,” I muttered.

Her eyes instantly lit up, like I had just given her the best gift in the world.

“Really?” she asked, stepping a little closer. I shrugged, trying to act like it didn’t matter. “Yeah. But don’t be annoying.” She nodded quickly, way too excited. “I won’t!”

I doubted that.

I didn’t know it yet, but that moment was the start of our friendship. She never left my side after that. Not even for a second.

She transferred to my school soon after and somehow ended up sitting right next to me, like it was already decided for her. She ate with me during lunch—even when I clearly didn’t want her to—and talked to me like we had been friends forever.

And then there was Astrid.

She met her for, what, five minutes? Maybe less. But somehow, they were already inseparable.

“Kenzy! This is Astrid!” she said one day, dragging the girl toward me with that same bright smile. “She’s my best friend!”

Then she paused, grinning even wider. “He’s my bestest friend.” I frowned at her. I never agreed to that. But for some reason… I didn’t correct her.

“Hi, Kenzy!” Astrid greeted me, way too excited for someone I had just met. My brows furrowed immediately. “It’s Kendrick, not Kenzy,” I corrected her flatly.

She blinked, clearly confused. “But Solenne calls you Kenzy.”

“Kendrick.” I didn’t even hesitate. “Oh—okay, Kendrick,” Astrid said, her voice turning a little awkward.

From the corner of my eye, I saw Solenne trying to hide her smile. I ignored it. There was no way I was letting two people call me that.

❦
“Kenzy, don’t forget your lunch!”

My mom’s voice echoed from the kitchen just as I reached the door. I stopped, letting out a quiet sigh as I waited for her to catch up.

She hurried over and handed me my lunchbox—then, without warning, pulled me into a quick hug and planted a kiss on my cheek.

Seriously? A kiss? I’m in high school now. I pulled back, giving her a look.

“Oh, come on. Don’t give me that glare,” she laughed. “You’re still my Kenzy boo!” I groaned, wiping my cheek like that would somehow erase it. “Stop calling me that.” She just smiled, completely unbothered.

“Have a nice day, Kenzy boo.” I shook my head, turning toward the door. Some things, apparently, were never going to change. “Bye, mom!”

I groaned, remembering it was my first day of senior high school. Great. I couldn’t wait to go home already. Though… I hadn’t even left our subdivision yet.

“Kendrick, susunduin ba si Solenne?” Kuya Paeng asked, glancing at me through the rearview mirror.

“Wait, po,” I said, fishing my phone out of my pocket. He nodded, patient as always.

I quickly typed a message to Solenne, asking her if she'd ride with us to school. Almost immediately, her reply popped up: Hi! Good morning, Kenzy! Yes, please! ><

I stared at the screen for a moment, blinking. Her excitement… It was infectious. But I chose to hide it. I shoved my phone back into my pocket, muttering under my breath, “Here we go again…”

“Kuya, makikisabay daw po si Solenne.”

“Sige, daanan natin siya,” Kuya Paeng smiled.

I looked outside the window, letting my eyes wander over the familiar streets of our subdivision. Houses passed by in a blur, kids going to school, neighbors greeting each other, and the occasional stray dog chasing its own tail.

We arrived at the familiar—big-ass—house with those massive windows. Honestly, you’d think the whole place was made of glass. Solenne was already waiting in the driveway, waving at me like I was some long-lost hero.

But then I noticed who was with her. Astrid.

Instantly, I closed my eyes. Great. Just great. This ride was already going to be long. And, judging by their energy, it was definitely going to be… annoying.

I muttered under my breath, “Perfect.”

Not that I don’t like them. It’s just… mornings are not my thing. The world moves too fast before I even have time to process it, and overly energetic people in the morning? Don’t even get me started. For some reason, it annoys the crap out of me. Every cheer, every bright-eyed “Good morning!” feels like a personal attack on my still-half-asleep soul.

But… they’re my friends. I like being around them. Most of the time. Okay, fine—I really like being around them. It’s just that my brain doesn’t function properly until at least ten in the morning, and apparently, they didn’t get the memo.

Ugh. That sounded… embarrassingly cringe. Why am I even thinking about this?

I’m supposed to be a cool, collected guy. Not some guy whining in his own head about friends being too happy.

“Good morning, Kenzy! Kuya Paeng!" Solenne's ever-energetic, impossibly loud voice probably reached the next ten houses. I groaned. Perfect.

Before I could even process it, Astrid jumped in with her signature enthusiasm: “Goooooooooooooood morning, po! Hi, Kendrick!”

I pinched the bridge of my nose and let out a long, defeated sigh.

Great. Today was going to be… fun. Or annoying. Or both. Definitely both.
