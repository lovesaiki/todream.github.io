Prologue
Sun Behind the Clouds
Solenne

Today’s the day.

I stood in front of the mirror, tracing the outline of my face with trembling fingers. My hands drifted down to my chest, feeling the rapid rhythm of my heartbeat. Each thump echoed louder in my ears, faster than the last, as if trying to warn me, or perhaps remind me, of the life I was about to step into. Tears threatened to spill over, blurring the reflection staring back at me.

Finally… I’m getting married.

“Solenne!” Astrid’s voice called from the doorway, warm and excited. “Let me see you!” I turned my gaze back to her, forcing a gentle smile through the storm of nerves and anticipation.

“Come on, Sid,” I whispered, my voice barely above the trembling of my own heart. The door swung open, and there she was—Astrid, radiant in the silk blue halter dress I had carefully chosen for her.

The fabric clung elegantly to her frame, shimmering slightly in the soft morning light. Her smile was wide, uncontainable, the kind that made her eyes sparkle with joy. Excitement danced across her face, and for a moment, I felt my chest tighten, a mix of comfort and bittersweet longing.

“Oh my gosh… I feel like crying,” she said, her voice exaggerated, dramatic, but full of genuine emotion. “You’re so beautiful, Sol. You’re absolutely dazzling!”

She stepped closer, moving carefully to my side, her eyes flitting down to make sure she didn’t step on the delicate tail of my dress. Every movement was measured, as if she feared breaking the fragile perfection of this moment.

“It fits you perfectly, babe,” she whispered, clutching her chest as though the sight of me overwhelmed her.

I shook my head, laughing softly, a warm smile tugging at my lips. “Ikaw ba ang ikakasal?”

She scoffed playfully, rolling her eyes, but unable to hide her happiness. “Duh! My best friend is getting married. I should be happy!”

Her grin was infectious, and I felt my chest swell—not just with nerves, but with the comfort of knowing she was here, sharing this moment with me.

Astrid is my best friend, my number one, my human diary, and my soul sister. She knows me better than I know myself sometimes—my thoughts, my fears, my ridiculous little habits—and somehow, she loves me anyway. She’s always been there, supporting every choice I’ve made, even the ones that, looking back, were clearly… questionable at best.

She has this way of advising with a mix of blunt honesty and tender care. She’d always warn me, “Don’t come running to me crying when something goes wrong,” yet the moment I do, she’s right there, arms open, ready to comfort me as if nothing else matters. She’s the kind of friend who celebrates your highs and softens your lows, all while making you laugh until your cheeks hurt.

With her, I can be completely, unapologetically myself—the happy, the scared, the silly, and the messy. And in this chaotic life of love, heartbreak, and now marriage, she’s the steady constant I cling to. Astrid is not just a friend; she’s the part of me that reminds me who I am, no matter where life pulls me. She’s irreplaceable.

She hurriedly grabbed her phone from her purse.

“Come on, let’s take a picture! The others are coming.”

We squeezed together, laughing as we managed a few quick selfies—and a few mirror selfies—before the rest of our friends arrived.

“Holy shit, Solenne!” Quincy exclaimed the moment he walked in, eyes wide, both hands flying to cover his mouth. From my perspective, you’d never guess he was a lawyer—he looked like a schoolboy caught off guard instead.

Behind him, the others appeared one by one. Claude stood stiffly at his side, his expression caught somewhere between disbelief and awe. Ivy, on the other hand… she was already crying.

Claude frowned, glancing at Ivy. “The wedding’s not even starting, what are you crying about?” Ivy shot him a look, tears streaking her cheeks. “Fuck you,” she snapped, voice muffled but full of emotion.

I stepped down from the wooden platform and eased onto the couch, my heart still racing. Quincy was immediately at my side, offering his arms like a protective older brother. The two girls hovered nearby, carefully adjusting the folds of my dress, making sure every crease was perfect.

“I can’t believe you’re getting married first,” Claude said, glancing at Ivy with a teasing smirk. “I always thought it’d be her.”

Ivy nudged him, cheeks flushed. “Shut up.”

Quincy leaned back, grinning. “You sure you two aren’t secretly together?”

Ivy rolled her eyes. “I’d rather marry an old man than marry him. Honestly, he acts like one anyway.”

Claude feigned offense, throwing his head back. “Damn you, lady.” But his tone was playful—I could tell he didn’t really mind.

Honestly, Claude did have a bit of an old-man vibe sometimes. Slow, sarcastic, and always sighing as if the entire universe is too loud, too fast, or too modern for him.

“Come on, why not just marry each other?” I teased, smirking at Ivy.

Astrid jumped in, grinning. “Yeah, and besides—why an old man? You said there’d be no difference anyway. Claude’s right there, you know.”

Ivy rolled her eyes so hard it was almost audible. “Why are we even talking about this nonsense? It’s Solenne’s day!”

“Right, picture time!” Astrid said, practically bouncing in her seat. She plopped down next to me, while Ivy claimed the other side, sniffling lightly but still managing a grin. Claude and Quincy stood behind us, pretending to be all serious and composed—though we all knew that act wouldn’t last long.

“Wait… where is he?” I asked, glancing around.

“Who?” Astrid tilted her head, feigning confusion, then realized. “Ah! Si Kenzy.”

“The groom’s room,” Quincy said without even looking at me, voice casual, like it was obvious.

Right. Where else would he be? My heart skipped a beat at the thought.

“Smile!” Astrid commanded, waving her phone in front of us.

We all grinned for the picture, the kind of smile that held excitement, nerves, and the chaos of the morning. Click. A moment captured—a memory of laughter, teasing, and the quiet thrill of the day I’d been waiting for.

After minutes of chitchat, laughter, and quiet tears, they finally left. The ceremony was about to start in just a few minutes. My heart pounded in my chest, thumping so hard I thought it might burst out at any second.

Breathe, Solenne. You’ve done scenes like this in movies before, I tried to tell myself. But no. This one was different. This was real. Like… this was actually happening. To me.

Holy shit. Holy fuck. Damn. Shit. Fuck. I kept muttering, hoping the words would somehow steady me. They didn’t.

And then… Here I was. Standing in front of the huge white doors, holding a bouquet of lilies, wearing my dream wedding dress—an elegant ivory silk off-shoulder mermaid gown that felt like it had been made for me alone.

Oh my god. This was actually happening. I’m getting married.

Tears welled up in my eyes before I could stop them, and I quickly looked down, swallowing hard. My hands tightened around the bouquet.

I gulped as the white doors slowly began to open. I lifted my gaze. Every eye in the room was on me. My throat tightened, and I let out a deep, steadying breath.

Then, slowly, I began to walk down the aisle. My gown’s train swept gracefully across the polished floor, brushing softly against the edges of the aisle, each step heavier and lighter at the same time. The scent of lilies mingled with the faint perfume of the guests, and the soft hush of the crowd made my heartbeat feel deafening.

This was it. This was my life in motion, unfolding like a dream I never dared to imagine… and yet, here I was, living it.

“My heart cannot take this, Hija. I can’t believe you’re getting married,” my dad said, his voice thick with emotion, tears glistening in his eyes. I laughed softly, trying—and failing—to hold back my own tears.

I turned to my mom and hugged her tight. “Oh, my dear Solenne,” she whispered, already crying. I held her for a moment longer, letting her warmth ground me as my heart raced.

My father gently took my hand, guiding me toward the front. Each step felt surreal, like walking through a dream I wasn’t ready for. And then… I saw him. Kendrick.

My chest tightened, my breath catching in my throat. My eyes met his, and the world seemed to shrink until it was only the two of us. His gaze held something I couldn’t name—familiar, electric, almost unbearably tender. My heart beat so loudly I was certain he could hear it.

I forced a smile, and he returned it, slow, steady, carrying a quiet intensity that made my stomach twist. I took my time walking down the aisle, each step measured. The train of my ivory silk gown swept softly across the floor, brushing past polished edges, and my bouquet of lilies trembled slightly in my hands.

This was my day. My life was changing. And yet, seeing him there, so close and impossibly present, I felt as though a part of my heart had always belonged to him—and still did.

He looked down for a moment, licked his lower lip, and for the briefest instant, I thought I saw something in his eyes… something that spoke of everything we had never said, everything we had never done.

The crowd watched, but all I could see was him. Every step I took was toward him, toward a life I had imagined countless times in secret. And in that moment, I believed, without question, that this was where I was meant to be.
